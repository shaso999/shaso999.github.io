Background

Guide to Seattle

Excerpted from "The Fire and the Future, Seattle in the 90s,and the decade that changed the world"

1994 Cadabra is Born

The second newest of the big four tech firms in Seattle, this retail giant was founded by Jeff & MacKenzie in 1994. While their original plan may have been to sell books, they would quickly seize on the wave of awakening in their backyard to propel their firm to the behemoth which runs half the worlds websites, and delivers more packages then the USPS, DHL, and UPS combined.

1995 IONS founded

The most recent, and possibly most influential of the big four, IONS was formally incorporated in 1995. It inherited the patents and groundbreaking work of Michael Bertiaux and Howard Rheingold, which would form the basis of both the awakening, magic, and modern cybernetics today. CEO Willis Harman then took the bold step of combining all of these patents into a single unified pool, with a fixed fee, open to anyone who wished to utilize the technology, setting the groundwork for the company’s future wealth, and making it into the R&D giant that continues to be integral to most advancements in technology today.

1996 The Father of All Demos

While the patents were filed and theoretically open to all a year earlier, it was this presentation to a UW auditorium in July of 1996 which would set off the boom that subsequently consumed the world. Over the course of an hour Willis Harman demonstrated both the finished Obsidian and Ruby implants, giving birth to the modern mages and hackers respectively, along with the first practical examples of cybernetic enhancement. While the broader public would remain skeptical of both the truth and usefulness of these discoveries for years to come, the local business community was sold, kicking off the first boom.

1999 United States v. Microsoft Corp.

While the case itself was quickly overshadowed by Black December, the division of Microsoft would have massive implications for the local economy, and create the big four as we know them today. No longer shackled by having to promote an operating system, the new Microsoft would rapidly expand into new areas of growth, launching new ERP, finance, and multimedia software, and becoming the omnipresent element of all of our lives. And Windows Corp, now left with one major stream of revenue, found itself forced to both expand its offerings for operating environments both larger servers, smaller mobile devices, and all manner of integrated applications in-between. Not to mention the launch of their Portal cloud environments, making them integral to the modern internet.

1999 1/12 (Battle for Seattle)

The WTO meetings in Seattle were contentious from the start. Even before 1/12 there was demonstrating, rioting, and pitched battles with police all around the sites of the meetings. It was becoming increasing clear that the police were losing control of the situation when Bill Clinton made the fateful decision to attend the sessions on 1/12 in person. As the president exited his convoy to a crowd of the worlds press, no one was expecting a man to suddenly appear behind him holding a sword, let alone for that man to decapitate the sitting president and disappear just as suddenly.

It would take nearly a week before it was clear that the anarchist and black block groups organizing the demonstrations were not responsible, but instead the awakened terrorists of the Invisible Committee. But in that time the reaction was swift and harsh, with thousands being held without trial, and nearly 10 blocks of downtown being heavily damaged or abandoned in the ensuing clean up operations.

1999 YTK

The city was still in the early stages of recovery from the battle of Seattle, when the final chapter of Black December came to a close. In a matter of minutes more then half the computers in the city were no longer operating, and 73% of the city found itself without power for nearly three days. While this further delayed the attempts to rebuild the city, more immediately this served as the final catalyst for President Gore to declare the war on terror, plunging the USA and Seattle into the 21st century.

Magic

Willis Harman’s Testimony to the Rehnquist Commission

What is magic? There are several things that this commission has asked me to explain or illuminate today in the light of recent tragic events but this seems to be the most fundamental. So what is magic? I could claim that its life, that its power, that its will and all of that is true, but as in many things my now deceased friend and colleague Michael Bertiaux put it best with his 11 Commandments:

  1. Magic Comes from Focused Will
  2. Magic is Fueled from Life
  3. Magic Takes Effort equal to its effect by the efficiency to the 8th power
  4. Magic Cannot Create more Life
  5. Magic Cannot Create a Soul
  6. Magic Cannot Manipulate Time
  7. Magic Cannot Remove Matter from Reality
  8. The Soul is Inherent to it’s form, to Change it is to Destroy it
  9. To Observe Something is to Change it
  10. A Soul Once Destroyed Cannot be Restored
  11. To Destroy the Soul is to Kill

Any question you have is answered there. Can you bring back the dead? No, nor can you speak or commune with them. Can you go back in time? No, with all respect to Marty McFly it’s functionally impossible, as is going forward or sideways, and attempts at prophesy or fortune telling also fail. What about teleportation? Well our friends at the postal service can rest easy because that to is beyond our keen. Well surely you say we can enchant an object, a sword or a mirror, or even just a broom? Alas no object has a mind of it’s own and without a mind to hold it the power just seeps away. And any attempt to ape Dr Frankenstein and make a golem, or to control others minds, or any other trick best seen in a Hammer Horror Film also do not work. Even attempts to directly heal the sick often are not possible. The body is a powerful thing, and while you can often restore something damaged, your body may well see Leukemia as working as intended.

Apart from those rules though, well you can do anything, if you have a discipline and the power. Move at impossible speeds, lift impossible objects, summon spirits to assist you, even Fly. Just don’t ask me to read your mind.

Savage Worlds Ruleset

This is the demo ruleset for running this setting in savage worlds.

Races

The races in this setting represent which of the awakened options your character has taken. Options are Hacker, Driver, Heavy, Mage, Summoner, Super, Regular.

Hacker

Where most people see the internet through screens and machines, you see the internet by jacking in, or for the newer kids getting close enough to a WIFI spot.

You are able to take the Hacking (Smarts) Skill.

You can take Hacker Edges

You have a derived stat 'Firewall', equal to 2 + Hacking/2


Driver

The average person these days relies on their car to drive itself, where you tell 20 cars where to drive themselves. Not only can you control multiple devices at the same time, you can also jump into a specific device and pilot it like you were there, useful assuming your smarter then whatever the lowest bidder wrote for the AI.

The total number of cars/drones/boats/devices you can control is theoretically unlimited, but you can only give direction to a number of groups equal to half your smarts die (so d6 is 3 groups)

You can also at any point jump into a specific device, in which case you may now roll and take all checks for the vehicle using your own stats and wild die, at plus 1.

You can take Driver Edges

Start with either the New Drones, or New Ride Edge


Heavy

Thanks to your awakening, your understanding of your own body has never been greater. When normal men cannot handle cybernetics which boost past normal human potential you are able to use them without any issues.

In combat deal two initiative cards to the Heavy, they act on both of them taking full turns.

Outside of combat, all tasks and movement take half as much time.

You can take Heavy Edges


Mage

Your a Wizard Harry… Ya wont be the first or last time you hear that, but being able to bend spacetime over your knee and make it cry uncle is one hell of a consolation prize.

You are able to take the Spellcasting (Smarts) Skill.

You can take Mage Edges


Summoner

You feel like your people have gotten a bad rap. The ability to summon and control creatures from beyond mortal keen has many uses, and the fact that every representation in the media is necromancer this, warlock that is hurtful. Sometimes you also bring out old ones.

You are able to take the Summoning (Spirit) Skill.

You can take Summoner Edges


Super

Have you ever wanted to stop bullets with your hand, fold into a suitcase, jump up 10 stories, make yourself invisible? Well all of those and more are possible if you can find your connection to the flow, and manifest it through your body. Just don’t try all four of those at once, we are still cleaning up bits of Fred.

You are able to take the Flow (Spirit) Skill.

You can take Super Edges

You start with the martial artist edge


Regular

You are just a normal person, no awakened powers. That said, no normal person would survive in this business, so you clearly have some incredible talents under the surface, that just required less invasive surgery.

You start the game with two additional advances, following normal rules.

Hacking

This section outlines how hacking works in this setting. This primarily impacts characters playing hackers, but has implications for the general way computer networks work.

Each computer, camera, server, device is a 'node'. If you control the node then you have access to all the data on it, and can give commands to it, and anything controlled by it. Each node has a number of 'connections' which link it to other nodes and the outside world. For example if you have a machine that controls security cameras, it might have a physical port that is a connection, a line connection to the server in the building, and a connection to the public internet. All abilities or attempts to influence a machine require you to use one of these connections. If it is physical, you need to be plugged in, or in wifi range, if it is to the net you need to have access to the internet, and if it is to another system, you need to either control or occupy that system. A hacker is in the most recent system they have operated on, without jacking out.

A hacker is in the most recent system they have operated on, without jacking out. So if I just hacked the camera computer, that is the system I am in. A hacker on their action can move between any controlled connected nodes freely before taking an action. If the hacker is acting normally (not in a trance) then at any time on their turn they can pull the plug and jack out, severing any connections. If they are instead in a trance, they have to get their conscious back to wherever they first entered from (physical jack, wifi, or public net). Assuming that path back is all along controlled systems they can act instantly. If it isn’t they have to spend turns gain access to the nodes in the way. Alternatively while in a trance, they can just jack out, but suffer 2D10 non-lethal fatigue damage. Also if they are ever incapacitated while being in a trance, they will also jack out immediately without further consequences.

Each connection is either bi-directional, or controlling. If the connection is bi-directional, you can get from either node to the other, and controlling the node on one side, does not help you control the other. If the connection is controlling, they one system is able to send orders to the other, meaning that if you control that node, you also can give orders to the downstream node, or access it without a check.

Each node or hacker that you try to act on has a firewall rating, which is the target number for most hacking actions. There are then modifiers depending on how you are accessing the node which are applied to your roll. See the following tables.

Each node can potentially have defensive software loaded on it. This is split into software which will always go off when a hacker is either in the node, or enters it (rare), and software that starting triggering once the alarm goes off (common). Each piece of software is a power, from the hacker powers list. It is casted using the hack skill of the system, with normal modifiers from the difficulty, but no penalties from failing to cast. A node cannot be a wildcard.

Certain powers generate noise, representing small errors and issues that system security will eventually pick up on. Noise is associated with the network of connected nodes that powers were used in. So if you cause noise in a system connected to 10 other nodes, the noise builds up for all of them. At the start of each turn for each network, roll a D10, if the result is equal or less then the amount of noise currently in the network, add one noise. If at the start of a turn the amount of noise is now 10, the alarm goes off, alerting any whitehats on the system, and broader corporate security. Note that if you cut off a node from other nodes and split a network in half, both parts inherit 100% of the original noise, but noise is only generated after the power takes effect. So if I split the camera computer from the broader network, it would still stay at 2 noise, if that was the level at the start of the turn. But if cutting the system off generated 2 noise, the systems that were linked before the power would not gain any.

Powers - Hacker

If you are a Hacker, this section details what powers you can use.

Your Arcane Skill is Hacking (Smarts). You start with the Control System power, and two additional starting powers of your choice.

When you first use a power on a system you have to decide if you want to go into a trance while hacking. If you do not, you are just using a device screen, and have awareness of what is happening around you. If you go into a trance you are fully in the system, and cannot see or notice things happening around your body. Each turn you are in a trance, you either decide to go cool or hot. If you are running cool, you apply -5 to all noise generated by the power to a minimum of 0. If you are running hot, you take 4 separate actions a turn, drawing 4 separate cards if in combat.

When you use a power apply the modifier on the power to your roll. If you get a raise on the check, apply -5 to all noise generated by the power to a minimum of 0. If you get a critical failure, the alarm on the network immediately goes off.

Powers - Super

If you are a Super, this section details what you can do with Magic.

Your Arcane Skill is Flow (Spirit). You start with 1, 5 point stance.

Unlike other casters, all of your powers manifest in your body, requiring far more thought and training to adapt your form to them, and stop them from conflicting. All of your powers exclusively either impact yourself, or have a touch range.

Instead of taking individual powers, you take combinations of powers called stances. For each stance select up to X power points of different powers. To activate a stance make a Flow (Spirit) roll, on a sucsess or fail they go into effect, but on a fail take one point of faitigue. On a critical fail take a point of faitigue and the stance does not work. On a raise, treat each component power as having gotten a raise.

You can only have one stance active at a time. Once a stance is active it will stay active until the user turns it off, the Super is no longer conscious/awake, or something breaks their concentration. At the start of a Supers turn, if they were shaken or stunned in the previous round they must pass a Flow (Spirit) test to keep control, otherwise their stance ends. Otherwise if an event occurs that would cause them to lose concentration the GM can require a new check.

The tn for any Flow (Spirit) roll related to a stance is the power points used in the stance.

Powers - Summoner

If you are a Summoner, this section details what you can do with Magic.

Your Arcane Skill is Summoning (Spirit). You start with 3 starting powers and 10 power points.

Every spell that you cast has the effect ‘PERMANENT’.

PERMANENT: Any creature summoned or controlled, will remain under your control until the creature is either incapacitated, or you decide to relinquish control. The power points used to summon/control it are “invested” and unavailable until you no longer control it. Once you no longer control it your power points begin to recharge normally.

Casting spells does not require a roll, unless otherwise noted. However to give orders to anything you control requires a Summoning (Spirit) roll with a TN equal to the power points invested in that entity. On a success they carry out the orders to the best of their ability. On a fail they carry out the orders to the best of their understanding.

The following are the spells available to be taken:

Control Dead

Rank: Novice, Power Points: 2 + 1/Zombie, Range: Spirit

Control dead grants animation and basic intelligence to the remains of a once-living being. The summoned horror is obedient but literal minded in its duties. It is controlled telepathically over any range.

Corpses aren’t summoned by this ability, so there must actually be a supply of bodies in Range. The brain must be intact. An undead Incapacitated by damage can’t be raised with this power again. Raising non-human targets does not work, outside of a few edge cases (African Greys, Corvids, Great Apes), where you can give very limited orders.

They have the following Stat Block:

Attributes: Agility/Strength/Vigor: Same as living - defaults to d6, Smarts d4, Spirit d4
Skills: All D4
Pace: 4; Parry: 4; Toughness: 7,
Special Abilities:

  • Bite/Claws: Str
  • Fearless: Zombies are immune to Fear and Intimidation.
  • Undead: +2 Toughness; +2 to recover from being Shaken; no additional damage from Called Shots; ignores 1 point of Wound penalties; doesn’t breathe; immune to disease and poison.
  • Weakness (Head): Called Shots to a zombie’s head do the usual +4 damage.

Dominate Animal

Rank: Novice, Power Points: SIZE of each creature controlled (min 1), Range: Spirit

This grants you the ability to take control of an animal. Attempts to control humans will fail since their minds are to complex. Once controlled you can give commands telepathically from any distance.

The SIZE of an animal is defined in the rules but for reference a wolf is -1, a Human is 0, a Lion is 1, and a Horse is 2. Also you can control swarms with small swarms being 1, medium swarms being 2, large swarms being 3.

You can control any creature with an animal smarts attribute (noted with ‘(A)’). If the creature has smarts of d4 or d6 resolve as noted above. If you try to control animals with near human intelligence (African Greys, Corvids, Great Apes), creature's with smarts of d8 or higher, on casting you need to pass a Summoning (Spirit) check against a TN equal to 1 + half their smarts (5-7) to take control. This TN is also the number of Power Points used, and the difficulty to give them orders. While not technically illegal doing this to a creature with near human intelligence will break their mind, in line with Humans.

The stats of creatures can be found in the rules, or extrapolated from those listings.
